Religion-based Politics in India

Early Inauguration of Ram Mandir

The Ram Mandir in Ayodhya has been a subject of political interest for decades. The early inauguration of the temple was strategically timed to garner votes for the upcoming elections. Political leaders aimed to use religious sentiments to attract voters. However, this move backfired when it became clear that the inauguration was more of a political stunt than a genuine religious event.

False Illusions in the Name of God

Politicians often create false illusions in the name of religion to manipulate public opinion. They promise divine intervention and blessings in return for votes. This not only undermines the sanctity of religion but also misleads the public. The use of religious symbols and narratives for political gain has become a concerning trend in Indian politics.

Division Based on Religion

One of the most troubling aspects of religion-based politics is the deliberate division of people along religious lines. Politicians exploit religious differences to create a sense of "us vs. them," leading to social discord and conflict. This divisive strategy not only harms social cohesion but also undermines the pluralistic fabric of Indian society.
