Indian Democracy vs Other Democracies

Overview of Indian Democracy

India is the largest democracy in the world. It has a federal structure with a parliamentary system, and its political framework is based on the Constitution of India, which was adopted on 26th January 1950. The President of India is the head of state, while the Prime Minister is the head of government.

Comparison with Other Democracies

India

  • Federal parliamentary democratic republic
  • Universal adult suffrage
  • Multi-party system
  • Secular state with no state religion
  • Regular elections with independent Election Commission

United States

  • Federal presidential democratic republic
  • Electoral college system for presidential elections
  • Two-party system (primarily)
  • Separation of church and state
  • Independent judiciary and frequent elections

United Kingdom

  • Constitutional monarchy and parliamentary democracy
  • First-past-the-post electoral system
  • Multi-party system
  • Established church (Church of England)
  • Regular elections and strong parliamentary traditions

Germany

  • Federal parliamentary republic
  • Mixed-member proportional representation
  • Multi-party system
  • Secular state
  • Frequent elections with a focus on coalition governments
